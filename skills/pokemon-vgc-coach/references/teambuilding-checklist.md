# VGC Teambuilding Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing or building a team. Not every box needs to be checked — this is a guide, not a rigid formula.

## Composition Check
- [ ] 6 unique Pokemon (no duplicate Pokedex numbers)
- [ ] 6 unique held items (Item Clause)
- [ ] All Pokemon legal in the current format/regulation
- [ ] Balance of ~4 offensive + ~2 support Pokemon
- [ ] Mix of physical and special attackers
- [ ] Every Pokemon has at least one damaging move

## Synergy Check
- [ ] Core Pokemon have clear synergy (type, ability, or move-based)
- [ ] No major anti-synergy (conflicting weather, self-damaging spreads hitting partners unnecessarily)
- [ ] Spread move safety: can your main attacker use its best spread move (Earthquake, Blizzard, etc.) without crippling its partner? If not, partners need immunities.
- [ ] Defensive type coverage — team isn't 4x weak to a common type
- [ ] At least one answer to each of the top 5 most popular Pokemon in the format
- [ ] If building around a specific combo: does every Pokemon on the team serve the combo's game plan, or are some just "good Pokemon" stapled on?
- [ ] If building around a gimmick (Skill Swap, Shed Tail, etc.): do you have at least two viable recipients/targets for the combo?
- [ ] Speed order check: for same-turn combos (Skill Swap + attack, Helping Hand + attack), is the support Pokemon faster than the attacker?

## Constraint Check (for gimmick/combo teams)
- [ ] Listed all hard constraints the core concept imposes on partners BEFORE picking Pokemon
- [ ] If using Illusion: all disguise-target partners have no visibly-activating abilities (no Intimidate, Defiant, Sand Stream, etc.)
- [ ] If using Earthquake spam: all field partners have Ground immunity
- [ ] If using a setup sweeper: every partner contributes to buying setup turns
- [ ] Team has at least one way to punish double Protect (Encore, Taunt, Nasty Plot, Swords Dance, or overwhelming offensive pressure)
- [ ] Identified the "star" (creates the advantage) vs the "win condition" (converts it into KOs) — they may be different Pokemon

## Speed Control Check
- [ ] Team has at least one form of speed control (Tailwind, Trick Room, Icy Wind, Thunder Wave, etc.)
- [ ] If running Trick Room: at least 2-3 Pokemon that benefit from it
- [ ] If running Tailwind: setter can reliably get it up turn 1
- [ ] Consider: can the team function if speed control fails?

## Offensive Check
- [ ] Team can threaten both physical and special walls
- [ ] Explicitly counted: at least 2 special attackers or mixed threats (don't end up with 4+ pure physical attackers)
- [ ] STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus) coverage hits most of the metagame for at least neutral
- [ ] At least one Pokemon can break through Protect stalling (spread moves, Feint, etc.)
- [ ] Team has a win condition — how does it actually close out games?

## Defensive Check
- [ ] Team isn't swept by a single common threat
- [ ] At least one Intimidate user OR other physical damage mitigation
- [ ] Answers to common status moves (Sleep, Burns, Paralysis)
- [ ] Can handle redirection (Follow Me / Rage Powder)

## Team Preview Flexibility
- [ ] Multiple viable lead combinations (not just one "correct" lead)
- [ ] Flexible bring-4 options against different team archetypes
- [ ] At least one Pokemon that's good in the back (can clean up late game)
- [ ] No Pokemon that's dead weight in >50% of matchups

## Metagame Awareness
- [ ] Checked Pikalytics for current top Pokemon and their common sets
- [ ] Team has a plan against the top 3 most popular team archetypes
- [ ] Considered how the team performs in Best of 1 (ladder) vs Best of 3 (tournament)
- [ ] If using uncommon Pokemon: identified their specific niche and why they're better than alternatives in that role
- [ ] Items mapped out early — no conflicts, and format-specific item availability considered (especially in Pokemon Champions where the pool is limited)

## Common Team Archetypes to Prepare For
- **Sun teams**: Torkoal/Drought + Chlorophyll sweepers
- **Rain teams**: Pelipper/Politoed + Swift Swim sweepers  
- **Sand teams**: Tyranitar/Hippowdon + Sand Rush sweepers
- **Snow teams**: Abomasnow/Snow Warning + Aurora Veil + Slush Rush sweepers
- **Trick Room**: Slow, bulky attackers under reversed speed
- **Hard Trick Room**: Dedicated TR with multiple setters and slow attackers
- **Tailwind Offense**: Fast Pokemon + Tailwind setter for overwhelming speed advantage
- **Hyper Offense**: Fast, frail, all-out attacking
- **Balance/Goodstuff**: Well-rounded teams with strong individual Pokemon

## Output Format
When presenting a completed team, always use Pokemon Showdown paste format:
```
Pokemon (M/F) @ Item
Ability: Ability
Level: 50
Tera Type: Type
EVs: X HP / X Atk / X Def / X SpA / X SpD / X Spe
Nature Nature
- Move 1
- Move 2
- Move 3
- Move 4
```

Include a brief team description explaining:
1. The core idea / win condition
2. How the 6 Pokemon work together
3. Suggested leads for common matchups
4. Which 4 to bring vs popular archetypes
